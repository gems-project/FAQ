## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q: What is the link between the GEMS project and Global Methane Genetics (GMG)?
**A:** Both projects focus on methane mitigation data, but with different scopes. The GEMS project is funded to concentrate only on historical data, while GMG mainly focuses on future data collection. To avoid duplication, save effort, and improve the efficiency of global methane mitigation efforts, GEMS participants are also offered the opportunity to link their data to GMG and join its data usage framework (data exchange standard). More information can be found [here](https://github.com/gems-project/Invitation-to-Contribute-Additional-Data/tree/main).

---

### Q: What technical safeguards are in place to protect the data, and who will have access?
**A:** All data are stored on secure, Cornell-managed cloud infrastructure, including Microsoft Teams, Azure Blob Storage, and Databricks. Data are protected using encryption, institutional authentication, and access logging.  
Access is role-based and restricted to approved consortium members, such as principal investigators, designated data managers, and authorized project staff (e.g. postdoctoral researchers), in accordance with the DUA. Downloading and data sharing are controlled, and data are not made public unless explicitly agreed by the contributing organization.

---

### Q: Can the governing law be changed from New York State law to another jurisdiction, or can the data be provided “as is” with no liability?
**A:** The Agreement must be accepted as written. Because the work covered by this DUA is administered and carried out at Cornell University, the Agreement is required to be governed by the laws of the State of New York. This provision cannot be modified, as it is part of Cornell University’s standard research DUA used across all partners.  
Data sharing under this Agreement is limited to a defined academic research project and is therefore considered low risk.

---

### Q: How is payment determined? Is it adjusted based on the number of studies submitted?
**A:** Payment is **not adjusted within tiers**. Any number of studies under five (Tier 1) receives a total payment of **USD 5,000**, following successful data-quality verification and payment eligibility review. The same fixed-payment principle applies to the other tiers as defined in the DUA.

---

### Q: Will our organization receive payment for providing data?
**A:** Each organization may choose whether or not it wishes to receive payment. If payment is elected, the amount is determined based on:
- the total number of studies contributed (Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3), and  
- whether all required **“Must Have”** data and any additional datasets committed in the study tables have been fully provided, as described in Section 11 of the DUA.

If payment is elected, it is issued as a single payment through the University’s procurement system after all submitted datasets have been verified for completeness.

---

### Q: What are the criteria for data-quality verification and payment eligibility?
**A:** Data-quality verification and payment eligibility require the provision of all **“Must Have Data”** fields that are pre-checked or required in the corresponding table(s), together with any additional complete datasets that the organization committed to provide. These requirements are specified in the tables included in the DUA.
